# What's new

A set of seven improvements — four to make daily work easier, three to keep your
money and records more accurate.

## Easier daily work

**Search your customers**
Type a name in the search box at the top of the Customers screen to jump straight
to that person instead of scrolling the whole list.

**Better item search when making a sale**
Searching for an item now understands the category, not just the name. Type a few
letters of the kind of item you want and the right ones come up first — no more
wading through unrelated matches.

**Warranty checking**
You can record warranty terms on an item (for example, "motor: 10 years, fan kit:
1 year") and the system remembers them on every sale.
*How to use it:* when a customer brings something back, open that sale and the
warranty screen tells you — for each part — whether it's still covered as of today
(or any date you pick). A fan's motor can show "still valid" while its fan kit shows
"expired", so you know exactly what you can honour.

**Overdue khata is highlighted**
You can put a "promised to pay by" date on a customer's khata. Once that date passes
and they still owe money, their name turns red on the Customers list so the people
who owe you and are late stand out at a glance.

## More accurate money and records

**Fix a wrong khata balance — the safe way**
If a customer's opening balance was typed wrong, you can correct it with a recorded
adjustment that shows in their khata with a reason — instead of quietly editing the
number. Important: a correction is *not* counted as cash received, so it never throws
off your daily cash total.
*How to use it:* open the customer, tap "Adjust khata", choose increase or decrease,
enter the amount and a short reason.

**Turn a quick-sale item into a real item**
When you ring up an uncatalogued item by just typing a name and price, you can later
turn it into a proper catalogued item (with its name and price filled in for you) so
future sales of it track cost and profit. It only sets things up going forward — it
never changes the old sale.

**Wire sold by the gaz, bought by the bundle**
For wire, you **buy by the bundle and sell by the gaz** — and every bundle is 90 gaz.
Mark a wire item as a bundle item, and the system does the maths:
- When you **buy**, you enter how many bundles and the price per bundle. It works out
  the cost per gaz for you.
- When you **sell**, you sell by the gaz as usual.
- **Prices are shown per gaz** (highlighted so they're easy to read), and your stock
  shows as bundles plus loose gaz — for example "4 bundles + 82.5 gaz".

Your profit on wire is now correct to the gaz, automatically.
